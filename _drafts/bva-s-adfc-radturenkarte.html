I bought those two maps for my ride between Copenhagen and Berlin in 2015 but I never really used them on the way, despite their quality. As many bike maps nowadays, those are waterproof.

This post is the first of - I hope - a series of similar one dealing with the construction of cycle maps. What do they look like? What are the convention used? What kind of information is represented? What kind of information is left aside? What are the difference between them? What is the scale? Etc.

We wil start today with the series ADFC-Radturenkarten by the German media company Bielefelder Verlag, often shortenned as BVA.

Detail of BVA's ADFC Radturenkarte near Rostock (Germany)

ADFC - or Allgemeiner Deutscher Fahrrad-Club in full length - is the German cycle federation, as is FUB in France or DFC in Denmark. They "work with cities, state and federal governments to improve the conditions for everyday cycling as well as cycling for tourists."
